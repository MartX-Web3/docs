The most common friction point in AI-driven DeFi is custody: either the agent holds your private key (dangerous) or you pre-deposit tokens into a smart account (inconvenient and capital-inefficient). Echo eliminates both problems using EIP-7702.

---

## The problem with pure ERC-4337

ERC-4337 account abstraction is powerful, but it introduces a structural issue for AI agents:

In standard ERC-4337, the `UserOp.sender` is a **Smart Account (SA)** ‚Äî a deployed contract, not your original EOA. When the Smart Account calls Uniswap, `msg.sender` at the Uniswap router is the Smart Account address.

Uniswap uses `msg.sender` to pull tokens via `transferFrom`. This means:

```
User EOA  ‚îÄ‚îÄtokens‚îÄ‚îÄ‚Üí  Smart Account  ‚îÄ‚îÄ‚Üí  Uniswap
                           ‚Üë
                    tokens must be here
                    before the swap
```

To execute a swap, you must first move tokens from your EOA into the Smart Account. Every time. This is the pre-deposit problem ‚Äî capital sits idle in a contract, not in your wallet.

---

## How EIP-7702 solves it

EIP-7702 introduces a new transaction type (type 4) that lets an EOA **temporarily install bytecode** at its own address via a signed authorization. Echo uses this to install `EchoDelegationModule` as the delegation target on your EOA.

After activation, your EOA address *is* the smart contract entry point. When a UserOperation is submitted with your EOA as `UserOp.sender`:

- `EchoDelegationModule.validateUserOp()` runs at your EOA address
- `EchoDelegationModule.execute()` calls Uniswap with `msg.sender = your EOA`
- Uniswap pulls tokens directly from your EOA via `transferFrom`

```
AI Agent
   ‚îÇ
   ‚ñº
UserOperation (sender = your EOA)
   ‚îÇ
   ‚ñº
EchoDelegationModule (installed on your EOA via EIP-7702)
   ‚îÇ  validateUserOp() ‚Üí checks policy + signature
   ‚îÇ  execute()        ‚Üí calls Uniswap
   ‚ñº
Uniswap Router
   ‚îÇ  msg.sender = your EOA
   ‚ñº
transferFrom(your EOA, pool, amount)
   ‚îÇ
   ‚ñº
tokens move directly from your EOA
```

No pre-deposit. No intermediate contract holding your funds. Tokens stay in your wallet until the moment of the swap.

## One-time activation

The EIP-7702 delegation is activated once via a **type-4 transaction** during onboarding. This transaction:

1. Signs an EIP-7702 authorization ‚Äî `{ chainId, address: EchoDelegationModule, nonce }` ‚Äî inside your mobile wallet
2. Includes that authorization in a transaction that calls `EchoOnboarding.registerInstanceAndEip7702()`
3. Registers your `PolicyInstance` on-chain at the same time

Once confirmed on-chain, the delegation code (`EchoDelegationModule`) is installed at your EOA address **permanently**. Future UserOperations do not need any EIP-7702 authorization attached ‚Äî the module is already there.

<Note>
  The delegation is scoped to the chain it was activated on. A Sepolia activation does not affect mainnet and vice versa.
</Note>

## Automatic token approvals

Before each swap, `EchoDelegationModule` calls an internal `_autoApprove()` function. This checks whether the Uniswap router already has sufficient allowance to spend your tokens. If not, it calls `ERC20.approve(router, type(uint256).max)`.

The behavior:

| Situation | Action | Gas cost |
|---|---|---|
| First swap of a given token | `approve(router, max)` | ~45,000 gas |
| Subsequent swaps of same token | No-op (allowance already max) | 0 additional gas |

By setting a max allowance on first use, Echo avoids approval transactions on every swap. The approve call is bundled into the same UserOperation as the swap ‚Äî it is atomic and never results in a dangling approval without a corresponding swap.

The `_autoApprove()` function supports all Uniswap V3 swap selectors:
- `exactInputSingle`
- `exactOutputSingle`
- `exactInput` (multi-hop)

## Security properties

Echo's architecture is designed so that even if the gateway is compromised, an attacker cannot exceed your policy limits:

- The execute key (stored encrypted in the gateway) can only sign UserOps
- Every UserOp is validated on-chain by `EchoPolicyValidator` before execution
- If the UserOp exceeds your daily cap, per-tx limit, or uses a non-whitelisted token, the validator reverts the operation
- The execute key **cannot** send regular ETH transactions or sign arbitrary messages ‚Äî it can only submit UserOps that pass policy validation
